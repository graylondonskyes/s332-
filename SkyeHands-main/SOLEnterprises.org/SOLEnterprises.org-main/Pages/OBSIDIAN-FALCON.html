Metric definitions & methodology (trust layer)
These definitions are included on purpose — clients trust what they can verify.
OTD: % of deliveries completed by the promised time window.
Method: delivered_on_time ÷ total_deliveries × 100.
OTIF: % of deliveries completed on time AND with full quantity.
Method: on_time_and_in_full ÷ total_orders × 100.
Dispatch time: request received → driver assigned (median or average).
Method: assign_timestamp − request_timestamp.
Claims rate: claims ÷ shipments × 100 (lower is better).

The OBSIDIAN FALCON Arizona

Arizona didn't need another logistics company. It needed one that could operate under pressure.

When The OBSIDIAN FALCON expanded into Arizona, the goal wasn't market presence—it was operational proof. Arizona is a stress test: wide metros, aggressive timelines, heat that punishes poor planning, and clients who don't have patience for excuses.

The OBSIDIAN FALCON’s Arizona division was built deliberately. We didn't flood the market with drivers or chase volume before control. We established a tight operational footprint and took on real work—same-day business runs, multi-stop route streams, and overflow coverage where failure was visible and costly.

Early Arizona jobs weren't trials—they were live operations. Office-to-office handoffs, time-sensitive pickups, and stacked routes running eight to twelve hours at a time. The OBSIDIAN FALCON didn't ask for patience. We delivered clarity.

As demand grew, standards tightened. Routes were structured to reduce dead time. Dispatch windows were defined instead of guessed. Drivers were briefed with expectations, not just addresses.

Arizona proved something important: Obsidian’s Logistics' model works in real conditions. Hellish heat. Wide geography. Tight windows. No margin for excuses.
