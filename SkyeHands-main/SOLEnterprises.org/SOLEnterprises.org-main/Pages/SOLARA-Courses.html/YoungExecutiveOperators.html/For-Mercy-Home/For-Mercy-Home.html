Host Compensation Model (Mercy revenue pool)

PER-STUDENT FEE + CAPACITY BONUS
Structure you requested: Skyes Over London pays Mercy Home an allowed per-student host fee for each paid seat in a cycle, plus a bonus percentage tied to class capacity. Flagship cohort is free and does not trigger host fees unless Mercy chooses optional support funding.
Suggested tier schedule (editable)
Tiered bonus example (based on fill rate = total seats used / capacity):
• Under 50% fill → 0% bonus
• 50%–69% fill → 2% of paid tuition
• 70%–84% fill → 4% of paid tuition
• 85%+ fill → 6% of paid tuition

You can raise/lower tiers to match Mercy’s hosting effort and internal policy.

MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING (MOU) — Mercy Home × Skyes Over London LC (Solara)

1) Parties
Host Organization: Mercy Home for Boys & Girls (“Host”).
Program Provider: Skyes Over London LC, operating Solara School of Intelligence (“Provider”).

2) Purpose
Host and Provider agree to deliver an on-campus Bridge to Success program as instruction/training for older youth/young adults, beginning with a flagship cohort, followed by repeatable paid cycles that include reserved Mercy Scholar seats.

3) Flagship Cohort (Cohort 0)
• Cohort size: 3–7 participants (or agreed size).
• Student cost: $0 (flagship cohort is in-kind / no participant tuition).
• Location: Mercy campus (scheduled sessions only).
• Purpose: proof-of-outcomes, operational playbook, and leadership reporting package.

4) Scaled Cycles (Cohort 1+)
• Seat price (public enrollment): $9,500 per seat (or agreed rate).
• Reserved Mercy Scholar seats each cycle: 3–7 seats at $0 to youth (funded via program model/underwriting).
• Mercy Scholars are not labeled, isolated, or treated differently in-session; they receive full experience and full credential eligibility gates.

5) Host Compensation (Scaled Cycles)
Provider will pay Host the following for each scaled cycle:
• Per-paid-seat host fee: $[X] per paid enrollment seat (excludes Mercy Scholar seats unless agreed).
• Capacity bonus: Host earns [tiered %] of paid tuition revenue based on class fill rate (or [flat %] as agreed).
• Payment timing: [deposit milestone / week 4 milestone / completion] as agreed in the final schedule.

6) Supervision + Safety
• Host provides supervised facility access and a designated point-of-contact presence during scheduled sessions.
• Host policies govern sign-in/out, room boundaries, device rules, and incident escalation.
• Provider delivers instruction and cooperates with Host policy enforcement and incident response procedures.

7) Participant Progression (Contractor Pool)
• Contractor pool eligibility may be offered as an optional progression for participants.
• For Mercy youth under Host care, participation in any contractor pool pathway is subject to Host sign-off/approval and any required guardian consent.

8) Media Capture (Photography/Video/Audio)
• Media capture is consent-gated and aligned with Host policy.
• Host may elect to use (A) Host’s internal media team, (B) Provider’s media team onsite, or (C) a hybrid model.
• No in-session recording outside approved capture windows and permissions.

9) Reporting + Evaluation
Provider will deliver leadership reporting: milestone updates (Week 3 + Week 8), deliverables completion metrics, credential issuance log, attendance summary, and incident summaries as permitted. De-identified reporting used where required.

10) Liability + Insurance + Non-Agency
Final agreement will define insurance requirements and any liability allocation. The parties remain independent entities. Nothing creates employment, agency, or partnership beyond the MOU scope.

11) Termination / Suspension
Either party may pause/terminate for safety, policy conflicts, or inability to perform. Participant removal may occur per Host policy.

12) Signatures
