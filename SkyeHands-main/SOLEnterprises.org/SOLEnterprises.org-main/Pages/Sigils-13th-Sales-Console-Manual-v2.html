1) Doctrine & Identity

What you are doing when you “sell”

You are not selling a service. You are not selling a campaign. You are not negotiating a deal. You are running an evaluation that moves a qualified operator into a founder-gated Seat Review process. Your “close” is not payment on the phone. Your “close” is the moment the prospect accepts the fixed ladder as policy, accepts standards gating, and chooses to submit for review.

Canonical definition (verbatim): “Sigils 13th is a 36-month Seat that installs business posture—Legitimacy, Enforcement, and Authority—measured by proof artifacts—under a founder-gated standard.”

Sigils language is posture language. You do not translate it into “branding and SEO.” You do not soften it into “a program.” You sell a Seat. You sell posture. You sell proof artifacts. You sell standards. If you drift from that, you create the wrong buyers.

Boundary line (say once, then move on)

We don’t guarantee outcomes we don’t control. We guarantee proof artifacts and installed posture.

2) Claim Guard & Brand Safety

Powerful language without liability

You are allowed to be extremely confident about what Sigils controls: inventories, artifacts, documented installs, sign-offs, proof. You are not allowed to imply control over revenue, rankings, virality, press, platform behavior, or third-party decisions. You are not allowed to present as legal representation. You are not allowed to discount or customize schedule. You are not allowed to say “approved.”

Founder-gated truth: You do not approve. You submit. You say: “Submitted for founder review / standards review.”

If the prospect requires guarantees, you do not debate. If they need guarantees to buy, they are not a fit. Selling guarantees is selling fantasy. Sigils is proof-based.

3) Objection Method (Enforced)

Policy → Reframe → Decision

Contractors fail because they answer objections like a vendor: long explanations, soothing, negotiating. That behavior trains the prospect to push harder. Sigils uses one method that prevents overtalk and forces decision. Every objection is handled with policy first, then reframe, then a decision question.

Method (always): Policy. Reframe. Decision question.

If the objection is price, your policy is the ladder and “no discounts.” Your reframe is the leak cost. Your decision question is structure vs cheap. If the objection is trust, your policy is proof artifacts and standards gating. Your reframe is that guarantees are scams. Your decision is posture yes/no. If the objection is time, your policy is that the seat requires discipline. Your reframe is that inconsistency kills businesses. Your decision is operator vs hobby.

One example (price)

Policy: Fixed ladder, no discounts. Reframe: your leak is already costing you monthly. Decision: do you want structure or do you want cheap?

5) Sigils Conversation OS

The four phases that produce clean submissions

Phase One is Control. You establish that this is not a vendor pitch. You time-box. You earn permission to diagnose. Phase Two is Diagnosis. You run the five questions as a leak audit and an operator test. Phase Three is Mechanism. You deliver one module that matches the leak they admitted. Phase Four is Commitment. You state the ladder as policy and close to Seat Review Submission.

Hard rule: Never pitch before diagnosis. Never price before fit. Never negotiate policy. Never chase.

If they cannot answer the questions, they are not a fit. If they negotiate schedule, they are not a fit. If they demand guarantees, they are not a fit. Protecting the roster is the product.
