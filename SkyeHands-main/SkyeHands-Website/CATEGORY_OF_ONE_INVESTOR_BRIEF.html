Category thesis

Skyes Over London did not enter a crowded point-solution market. The SkyeHands ecosystem unifies a signed event bus, a 13-brain AI agent mesh, a governed AI routing gateway (kAIxUGateway13), SkyeCards economy, ValleyVerified procurement/fulfillment network, multi-tenant SaaS infrastructure, 72+ products spanning document editing, lead automation, email, commerce, scheduling, CRM, sports, gaming, knowledge management, and AI training infrastructure — all under a proof-discipline governance system in one operator-owned surface. No single competitor ships all seven layers together. That is the Autonomous AI-Operator Platform thesis in product form.

The 13-brain AE mesh alone is architecturally novel: each brain has a distinct role (legal, finance, commerce, media, etc.), role-scoped permissions, provider fallback chain, and max-hop loop protection — not a generic "assistant" but a specialized team of agents with explicit accountability boundaries. kAIxUGateway13 layers governed AI routing, per-device billing, and cohort analytics on top — something no single SaaS product in the market currently ships as one coherent platform.

Why this is not a toy

  • The Platform Bus uses SHA-256 signed envelopes, nonce-based replay protection, and an immutable NDJSON audit ledger — not a simple pub/sub queue.
  • JobPing is a fully deployable near-term revenue platform: Next.js 15, Prisma migrations, Vitest test suite, verified Stripe webhooks, Twilio consent tracking, and niche template packs. Only missing: production env vars.
  • JobPing is now bound into AE Flow through a dispatch adapter for restaurant coverage, open-house staffing, priority lead routing, and appointment follow-up.
  • SkyeCards turns SkyGate into an in-house usage economy: Stripe and PayPal mandates, $50 starter usage, monthly AI/product credits, token and push buckets, product-line credit expiry rules, and paid revenue packs are implemented in gateway code.
  • SkyeDocxMax is production-ready today — a complete offline-first encrypted PWA with Web Crypto API, IndexedDB, and Service Worker caching. No backend required.
  • kAIxUGateway13 is a full-stack governed AI routing platform with device key billing, Stripe integration, Neon database, Redis caching, 40+ Netlify Functions endpoints, and cohort analytics — the kind of infrastructure that normally takes a team 12+ months to build.
  • SkyeSuite ships 9 distinct productivity apps (document editor, cloud drive, workflow, ledger, ops console, sheets, slides, archive, collab) all unified under one gateway — a full competitor to Google Workspace's scope, not a single app.
  • SkyDexia AI Brain Drive provides a 90GB Docker/GPU training and RAG platform with Memory Fabric v6.8.1 — persistent distributed memory for all 13 AE brains, without reliance on any external vector service.
  • The multi-tenant schema is tenant-scoped from ground up — no retrofit required at scale. RBAC with five roles enforced at the schema level, not the application layer.
  • Proof discipline: every claim has a smoke script, every smoke generates a proof file, GrayChunks scanner enforces 13 rules, and the release gate blocks promotion on missing proof. Status reports are code-backed.
  • The dev infrastructure includes a fully wired Dev Container (Ubuntu Noble), local AI (Ollama Qwen Coder 7B), and Playwright browser smoke tests with repo-local Chromium — no external dependencies to start developing.

Investor reading

The $86.8M code-floor valuation is the present-value read on the existing repository as an integrated operator platform — not a revenue multiple, not a customer multiple, and not a speculative hype premium. The $115M–$190M pre-money raise posture reflects how an informed investor can price a category-creating platform with 72+ products, a governed AI routing gateway, a 13-brain agent mesh, a canonical procurement/fulfillment network, and live proof discipline. The $600M–$3.2B strategic upside is separated intentionally: it belongs to the post-commercial-proof acquisition case, not the current code-floor. The remaining completion work is provider-variable and deployment execution, not product discovery.
